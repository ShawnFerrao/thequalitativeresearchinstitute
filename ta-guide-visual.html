Coding Reliability TA is designed for studies where consistency is the central value. It is most often used when multiple coders need to apply the same codes in the same way, when qualitative data are being categorised systematically, and when funders, ethics boards, or journals expect reliability as a quality marker.

In this approach, the researcher is expected to apply codes as consistently and neutrally as possible. Subjectivity is treated as something to manage or reduce — not a resource to draw on. Frameworks are typically developed before or early in the analysis, then applied rigorously to the data.

Themes are
Topic summaries — categories that organise what participants talked about
Coding is
Systematic application of a predefined or early-developed codebook
Rigour looks like
Inter-rater reliability scores, structured processes, documented consistency
Best suited to
Multi-coder studies; mixed methods; contexts requiring replicability
Watch for: If you are using inter-rater reliability while claiming to do Reflexive TA, that is a mismatch. These approaches are not compatible. Reflexive TA explicitly does not use coding reliability as a quality marker.

Codebook or Coding Frame TA sits between highly structured and highly interpretive approaches. It is designed for studies where a shared framework is useful but interpretation still matters. The codebook may begin with a predefined structure or develop inductively from the data — and it can be refined as analysis progresses.

This approach is common in applied, policy-focused, evaluative, and team-based research. Researchers use interpretation and professional judgement, but within an agreed analytic structure. Transparency and a traceable decision trail are what make this approach rigorous.

Themes are
Patterned responses mapped across cases, groups, or the dataset
Coding is
A shared, structured process that may adapt as analysis develops
Rigour looks like
Clarity, transparency, auditable analytic decisions, consistency of process
Best suited to
Team research, applied settings, evaluation and policy projects
Watch for: If your coding is entirely flexible with no shared structure, or if you are working solo on interpretive or conceptual research, Reflexive TA may be a better fit. If you are seeking inter-rater reliability as your main quality marker, Coding Reliability TA is more appropriate.

Reflexive TA is designed for studies where interpretation and the researcher's analytic role are central. Themes are not inputs into analysis — they are outputs. They are patterns of shared meaning that you actively generate through deep, sustained engagement with the data. They are not discovered; they are constructed.

In Reflexive TA, the researcher's subjectivity is not a problem to manage — it is a resource to engage with thoughtfully. Reflexivity is not an add-on or a quality statement at the end of a methods section; it is woven through the entire analytic process. This approach does not use coding reliability, consensus coding, or fixed codebooks as markers of quality.

Themes are
Patterns of shared meaning actively generated through interpretation
Coding is
A flexible, evolving, interpretive process — coding produces insight
Rigour looks like
Reflexivity, interpretive depth, coherent and clearly explained analytic choices
Best suited to
PhD research, solo interpretive studies, experience and meaning-focused work
Watch for: If you are using a fixed codebook, seeking inter-rater reliability, or working in an applied context that requires an auditable process, Reflexive TA is not appropriate — even if it sounds appealing or is commonly cited in your field.
Common pitfalls

Signs your approach needs clarification

These are not signs your study is flawed. They are signs your analytic logic needs to be made more explicit — and can usually be resolved through a clearer articulation of your choices.

Mismatch
Using inter-rater reliability while claiming to do Reflexive TA
Inter-rater reliability is appropriate for Coding Reliability TA — it is explicitly not a quality marker in Reflexive TA, where researcher subjectivity is a resource, not a problem.
Mismatch
Calling themes "patterns of shared meaning" but treating them as topic summaries
Language borrowed from Reflexive TA with practices from Coding Reliability TA creates incoherence that reviewers and examiners will notice.
Mismatch
Using a fixed codebook while describing coding as reflexive and organic
A fixed codebook is a feature of Coding Reliability or Codebook TA. Reflexive TA coding is flexible and evolving — the two are not compatible in the same study.
Mismatch
Claiming inductive analysis while applying a predefined framework
Inductive and deductive are not the same as flexible and structured. Clarify whether your framework was developed from the data or applied to it.
Mismatch
Receiving conflicting advice from supervisors or reviewers
This often means different people are assuming different TA approaches. Making your approach explicit — and why it fits your study — usually resolves the confusion.
Mismatch
Feeling unsure how to justify your analytic choices in writing
If you cannot complete the sentence below, your approach likely needs clarification — not more data or more codes.
Putting it together

Can you complete this sentence?

When you can write this clearly, your approach is coherent. If you get stuck, that is useful information — it points to exactly what needs clarification.

I am using type of TA to analytic aim because reason it fits. This means I understand themes as definition, coding as function, and quality as criteria.
Coding Reliability TA
"I am using Coding Reliability TA to systematically categorise participant responses because consistency across coders is essential for our multi-site study. This means I understand themes as categories, coding as a structured and consistent process, and quality as inter-rater reliability."
Codebook / Coding Frame TA
"I am using Codebook TA to map patterned responses across policy contexts because the research needs to be auditable and usable by stakeholders. This means I understand themes as patterned responses, coding as a shared evolving framework, and quality as transparency and traceable decisions."
Reflexive TA
"I am using Reflexive TA to interpret the meaning participants make of their experiences because conceptual insight matters more than categorisation. This means I understand themes as patterns of shared meaning I generate, coding as a flexible interpretive process, and quality as reflexivity and depth."
